Hello!

Interactive storytelling that is perfect for kids from 6-14 years old.
Practice your child's vocabulary and enjoy interactive storytelling
that will bring them to the fantasies of Filipino stories! Explore
three Filipino stories and learn while dragging and clicking on
different games! So let's go. HAMPANG now!

... HAMPANG- a Hiligaynon term for the word "play" because we want
kids to have a unique experience while learning in a gamified way.
Featured here are three Filipino short stories that will help your
child learn life lessons that they could bring into their everyday
success. Kids can also enjoy two different games that will make
learning easier by just clicking and dragging. Dyslexic-friendly
interactive storytelling that will show the wonders
of Filipino stories.

Who we are

We are students from Mapua Malayan Colleges Mindanao
majoring in Bachelor of Multimedia Arts that conducted a thesis
study entitled: HAMPANG: An Interactive Storytelling for Students
with Dyslexia. This study aims to help create interactive storytelling
as an alternative reading material for students with Dyslexia.

    Once there was a deer who is very
  proud of himself. While walking, he
  saw a snail moving slowly.
    The deer challenged
  the snail into a race.
    The snail went
  home and told
  the other snails
  about the race.
  They made a plan
  to outsmart
  the deer.
    When Sunday came, they
  took up hiding places a little
  distant from each other. All
  the way from the starting
  point to the finish line.
    Then the race started.
    During the race, the deer tried
  to increase his pace, but the
  snail is still ahead of him. His
  sweat spilled, his legs felt weak
  and his breathing panted.
    The deer kept running, until he
  finally saw the finish line. He
  felt happy, because he thought
  that he won the race.
    How surprised the deer was
  because he saw the snail sitting on top
  of the rock near the finish line.
    He approached the snail and bowed
  his head in recognition of defeat.
     MORAL OF
  THE STORY

Always try to be the best, but never
underestimate other's abilities.

    One day, a monkey and a turtle
  were hungry and saw a banana
  tree floating in the river.
    They decided
  to divide the
  banana tree
  into two and
  plant it.
    The monkey kept teasing
  the turtle that his tree
  would not grow.
    They went to visit the tree
  that they had planted.
   
  The monkey was shocked
  and jealous because the
  turtle's tree grew into a
  banana tree while his
  tree stayed the same.
    The monkey climbed
  the turtle's tree and
  ate some bananas.
  The turtle was angry
  and yelled at the
  monkey.
    Since the turtle cannot climb
  the tree, he placed thorns
  around the tree to teach
  the monkey a lesson.
    Monkey fell down the tree
  and was hurt by the thorns.
  He decided to take revenge
  on the turtle.
    The foolish monkey did exactly
  that; he picked the turtle up
  and threw it into the river.
    The turtle gladly came up to the
  surface of the water and
  mocked the monkey.
    As for the banana tree? Well, the weeds
  took over the tree, for the monkey was
  too foolish to know how to take care of it.
  Since that day, monkeys and turtles
  have been bitter enemies.
    MORAL OF
  THE STORY

Do not be greedy, always
show kindness.
